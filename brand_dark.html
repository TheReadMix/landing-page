Palette Inspiration Black and white nods to the composition notebook — the universal symbol of student writing. Red, white, and blue echoes the American flag — a reminder that literacy is not just an educational issue, it is a national one. The ReadMix palette holds both without executing either literally.
Typography Direction
The ReadMix
Wordmark & Headings — Barlow Condensed 800
Tight, tall, commanding. Magazine masthead energy. Stops you mid-scroll.
Struggling readers don't need sympathy. They need a system that works — built for the way they actually learn.
Body — Inter Regular 400
Legible at every size. Approachable without being soft.
Avoid: decorative fonts · script typefaces · slab serifs · anything that reads like a school newsletter from 2003
Compositional Rules
01 — One dominant elementEvery layout has a clear visual anchor. No visual tie games.
02 — Asymmetry with intentionLayouts feel dynamic, not chaotic. Off-center is okay. Unbalanced is not.
03 — Let black breatheDark backgrounds are stages. Give elements room to perform.
04 — Type as design elementHeadlines can crop. Letters can anchor a layout. Words carry visual weight here.
05 — Colour lands like a punchRed and cyan are deployed, never decorative. Sparingly used so they always land.
06 — Grid is the backbone, not the cageStructure is followed — and broken when rhythm demands it.
